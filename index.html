Polona Frelih is an award-winning journalist and a Moscow correspondent of Slovenian daily Delo.

Polona Frelih studied journalism in Ljubljana University (Slovenia) and Roosevelt University in Chicago (USA). After graduation in 1997 she started her professional journalist career at Slovenian National TV. Since 2007 Frelih has been Moscow correspondent of the Delo Daily, a major newspaper in Slovenia. She was also the author of the weekly review of world events called the Weekly Mirror and a Slovenian correspondent for the CNN World Report.

Frelih has been following political, economic, cultural and everyday life in Russia and other republics of the former Soviet Union traveling extensively across the CIS. In 2008 Frelih reported breaking news on the 2008 Georgian-Russian conflict from the border between South Ossetia and Georgia. In 2010 she received a professional award for her piece on Belorussian youth struggling their life in "Europe's last dictatorship".

In July 2013 Frelih reported from the closed doors meeting with whistleblower Edward Snowden at Moscow International Airport Sheremetyevo. She was the only journalist present at the meeting between Snowden and several representatives of human rights organizations.
